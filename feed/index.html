src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/7.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>Having breakfast in nature is an amazing feeling</figcaption> </figure> <p>At home, we normally have eggs in any form, but it’s not very practical to take them on the trail so we tried variations of porridge and oats — without much success as we felt hungry soon. Breakfast was an issue for us until we discovered these <a href="http://backcountrycuisine.co.nz/bcc/index.php/archives/223">freeze dried scrambled eggs</a>. Surprisingly, they are made out of normal ingredients and they taste nice. We like to mix them with a bit of leftovers from dinners — usually plain couscous or mashed&nbsp;potatoes.</p> <h2 id="lunch">Lunch</h2> <p>We love <a href="http://shop.countdown.co.nz/Shop/ProductDetails?stockcode=289839">walnut oat crackers by 180 degrees</a> with an aged cheddar – amazing taste and lasts forever. It’s a nice easy meal that can be spread over multiple stops and you don’t have to set up your gas&nbsp;burner.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/6.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="Munging through those cheese and crackers" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/6.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>Munging through those cheese and crackers</figcaption> </figure> <p>Sometimes we carry an extra stick of Hungarian-style salami, but mostly we are fine with the vegetarian&nbsp;option.</p> <h2 id="snacks">Snacks</h2> <p>We carry three different types of snack but really it’s up to you and what you like. First, we have a mix of natural nuts – usually cashews, almonds, Brazil nuts, Macadamian and&nbsp;walnuts.</p> <p>Instead of commercially produced muesli bars that are full of sugar and god knows what, we <a href="http://www.wildbackpacker.com/backpacking-food/recipes/backpacking-lunch-snack-recipes/">make our own</a>. And it is surprisingly tasty and easy to make – you don’t even have to bake. Mix three cups of quick oats with a cup of heated peanut butter and add up to a cup of honey. Stir everything together (you can add cranberries, raisins, chopped almonds or anything you like), press it into a box container and put into the&nbsp;fridge.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/4.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="After a big hike you need to keep your energy levels high" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/4.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>After a big hike you need to keep your energy levels high</figcaption> </figure> <p>The third to the party is a pack of dried dates. Dates are delicious, more nutritious than candy and helps with your sweet&nbsp;tooth.</p> <p>Oh, and a small piece of dark chocolate is always a welcoming surprise after a tough&nbsp;climb.</p> <h2 id="dinner">Dinner</h2> <p>Dinner is usually your biggest meal of the day on a hike as you have time to prepare it and you don’t have to move afterwards. For a track this long, you don’t have to be too creative and meals can be quite simple. I’m a fan of freeze dried ingredients as their are light, but not a fan of instant meals as they usually contains lots of things that are bad for&nbsp;you.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/2.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="Fine dining in a hut" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/2.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>Fine dining in a hut</figcaption> </figure> <p>My favorites are <a href="http://backcountrycuisine.co.nz/bcc/index.php/archives/226">freeze dried minced beef</a> as a good source of protein and <a href="http://backcountrycuisine.co.nz/bcc/index.php/archives/242">vegetable mix</a> (carrots, peas, corn). We usually get a pack of 160 g of beef which lasts us for the whole trip and the same applies to&nbsp;vegetable.</p> <p>These two things create a base that can be combined with couscous for one day and instant mashed potato flakes for the&nbsp;other.</p> <p>Pro tip: Use dehydrated milk for your mashed potatoes to make it taste like&nbsp;heaven.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/3.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="Sandwich plastic bags are handy to store portions and save on weight" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/hiking-food-for-a-3-day-track/3.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>Sandwich plastic bags are handy to store portions and save on weight</figcaption> </figure> <p>Also, carry a small plastic bottle of olive oil and add couple spoons to each meal to boost the calories and fat content.
