There is a number of short sidewalks which are definitely worth stopping such as The Mirror Lakes or The Chasm but just drive itself through the glacial Eglinton Valley is&nbsp;spectacular.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/1.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="A view across Eglinton Valley" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/1.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>A view across Eglinton Valley</figcaption> </figure> <p>Once we reached the Homer Tunnel, the traffic started to be quite dense as the tunnel was only one way and operated by a traffic light. I was at the place about 10 years ago and I don’t remember such a&nbsp;buzz.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/4.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="Heading off to the Tasman Sea" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/4.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>Heading off to the Tasman Sea</figcaption> </figure> <p>While waiting for our cruise, we made an easy 20 minute loop through beech forest and along shore with some nice views of the fjord and surrounding peaks. Luckily, our skipper didn’t let us suffer too long and after few minutes turned the boat back to the harbour. In the morning, we were a bit in a rush but in the afternoon, on the way back, our driver made a few stops along this section, so we had enough time to wander around and enjoy all&nbsp;views.</p> <figure> <a href="https://barakuba.com/photos/960x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/11.jpg" class="gallery-link"><img alt="The silence and calm were amazing" src="https://barakuba.com/photos/640x/milford-and-doubtful-sound/11.jpg" /></a> <figcaption>The silence and calm were amazing</figcaption> </figure> <p>The second part of the day started at Dove Cove where we got on board and cruised again about 30 km to the open Tasman Sea.
